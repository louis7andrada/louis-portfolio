About

Bio

Louis Andrada is a Brazilian painter based in Toronto. Born and raised in Brazil, he relocated to Canada at 19, carrying with him a cultural foundation shaped by Brazilian folklore, symbolic narratives, and a lifelong fascination with horror films and stories. He is formally trained in film photography and photo production, with a grounding in analog processes and darkroom techniques. This technical training informs his approach to image construction, material control, and visual structure within his painting practice.

Andrada’s work bridges his photographic background with painting, developing a distinct visual language rooted in tonal exploration and fragmentation.

Statement

Louis Andrada’s practice merges the macabre with the technical rigor of film photography printing. Drawing from analog darkroom processes, he translates grain, chemical contrast, half-tone structures, and disrupted resolution into paint. His images often feel as though they have been developed rather than composed, emerging through layers that mimic photographic exposure and mechanical breakdown.

Influenced by Brazilian folklore, occult symbolism, and a lifelong immersion in horror narratives, his work confronts fear directly. Forms surface and dissolve within a single frame, suspended between clarity and decay. The resulting fragmentation evokes instability, as if the images resist full visibility.

He paints what unsettles him. Each image is an act of confrontation, rendering scenes that, if encountered in reality, would provoke fear, distress, or torment. Through paint, he fixes the uncanny in place. His work functions as containment: an effort to materialize fear and confine it to the canvas, ensuring it exists there and nowhere else.
